DUSTER

Data Upload Service for Translational rEsearch on Redcap

Preamble

Welcome to Stanford REDCap's DUSTER, a self-service tool to automatically import clinical data associated with research subjects in your study.

DUSTER has both patient data and structured clinical data, which includes risk scores, lab results and medications as well as inpatient stay metrics such as blood products, LDA, IO and ventilation.

You can use DUSTER either to augment an existing REDCap or to populate a newly created REDCap project.

Once you have specified the clinical variables of interest, DUSTER will automatically import all data relating to your patients once a day.

DUSTER expects you to create records containing an MRN and date of enrollment. These two fields in REDCap are then used to retrieve associated patient and clinical data for the patient in the specified time frame(s).

This version of DUSTER only supports classic study design. Longitudinal study design support is planned for a future release.

Please note that because we are pulling clinical data from the EMR, we require that you store a patient identifier in your REDCap project. You can use the PHI masking and user role features of REDCap to ensure that only the minimum necessary PHI is viewed by the study team.

Step 1 of 4: Identifying Your Cases

Clinical data is always associated with (1) a patient, and (2) a date and time of day.

In order to automatically import clinical data, you must first supply the patient identifier and date or date and time of the defining event for your study.

The identifier is almost always the Stanford Medical Record Number (MRN) but may in some cases be another unique identifier such as a radiology accession number. At this time we only support Stanford MRN.

The date is generally either the date of study enrollment or the date of the baseline study visit.

Design Review and Cleanup

As an optional final step you can now reorganize and prune your variables by grouping them into instruments and removing unwanted fields.

You can edit the instrument names, introduce new instruments, and delete extraneous fields at any time using the Data Dictionary. Be aware however that if you change any of the field names ending in "_dstr" you will no longer get data in the renamed fields.
